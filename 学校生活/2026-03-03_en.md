## Phonics

**Red Group** focused on the letter sounds p and d. The children practised identifying words beginning with these sounds, distinguishing them from previously learned letters, and completing writing practice. They then completed a blending and segmenting activity, identifying and ordering sounds within given words before reading them together to check accuracy.

**Green Group** learned the new Set 2 sound "oo", using the phrase "oo, poo at the zoo." They explored the words zoo, moon, spoon, pool, and food, focusing on hearing and blending the long "oo" sound accurately. The group reviewed previously taught sounds and green words, read nonsense "alien" words to strengthen decoding skills, and revisited red sight words (I, the, your, he, of, no, put, said) for quick recognition. They then practised spelling "oo" words using magnetic boards and completed handwriting worksheets to consolidate spelling and letter formation.

**Orange Group** reviewed previously learned sounds and blended familiar words confidently. They then focused on the next Magic E pattern, the o-e sound, reading and saying related words while using "Fred in their Head" to support independent blending. The session concluded with reading this week's book, A Bad Fright, working carefully through each page with strong focus and effort.

**EYFS Benefits:**
- Literacy: Developing decoding, blending, and spelling accuracy
- Communication & Language: Articulating sounds clearly
- Physical Development: Strengthening handwriting skills
- Characteristics of Effective Learning: Applying phonics strategies independently

## Self-Exploration Time

The classroom offered a range of hands-on activity stations:

- **Arctic Insulation Experiment** – Children placed their hands in an ice box and used cotton wool inside ziplock bags as "gloves" to understand how insulation helps Arctic animals stay warm.

- **Biome Sorting Tuff Tray** – A mini-world with seven biomes representing each continent allowed children to sort plastic animals into their correct habitats.

- **Animal Pattern Painting** – Children carefully observed animal fur patterns and recreated them on coloured paper.

**EYFS Benefits:**
- Understanding the World: Exploring habitats, climates, and adaptation
- Expressive Arts & Design: Observational painting and pattern awareness
- Communication & Language: Explaining reasoning and observations
- Physical Development: Fine motor control through painting and sorting

## Miss Naomi's Chinese Class

The children continued the spring theme by learning new vocabulary: 小鸟 (bird), 蝴蝶 (butterfly), 毛毛虫 (caterpillar). They practised the sentence structure: "我看到一只……" (I see a …)

The lesson focused on using correct measure words, especially 一只 (for animals) and 一棵 (for plants). By linking previous vocabulary with today's learning, the children began to understand that different objects require different measure words. During the literacy activity, the children used familiar Chinese character cards to build complete sentences such as: "我看到一只小鸟。"

**EYFS Development Links:**
- Communication & Language: Expanding vocabulary and speaking in full sentences
- Literacy: Recognising characters and forming simple sentences
- Mathematics: Developing classification awareness through correct measure words
- Characteristics of Effective Learning: Making connections between previous and new learning
