## Phonics

**Red Group** began by reviewing previously learned letter sounds and practising blending together as a class. The children then split into two smaller groups. One group focused on blending activities using magnetic letters to segment, blend, and spell simple words. The second group worked on reading the book Put It On, practising blending and segmenting three and four letter words to improve fluency while also answering simple comprehension questions.

**Green Group** learned the Set 2 sound "ar." Using the focus rhyme "ar, start the car," the children practised reading the words part, car, card, and star, identifying the "ar" sound and blending it within words. They reviewed previously taught sounds and green words, read "alien" nonsense words linked to "ar," and revisited their red sight words (I, the, your, he, of, no, put, said, my). During the spelling section, the children listened as the teacher sounded out "ar" words and used Fred Fingers to count the number of sounds before writing the words in their handwriting books. The correct spelling was then shown so the children could check and correct their work. The class also read through their weekly book again to build familiarity and confidence with the text.

**Orange Group** focused on the "aw" sound, as heard in words such as yawn and dawn. The children practised reading words including yawn, dawn, straw, and paw, demonstrating increasing confidence with this longer vowel pattern. They also completed writing practice by spelling words containing the "aw" sound. The session concluded with both group and independent reading of this week's book, A Bad Fright, allowing the children to apply their phonics skills while reading a complete text.

**EYFS Benefits**

✓ Literacy: Developing decoding, blending, and spelling accuracy
✓ Communication & Language: Articulating sounds clearly and improving reading fluency
✓ Physical Development: Strengthening handwriting and fine motor skills
✓ Characteristics of Effective Learning: Applying phonics strategies with increasing independence

## Chinese Class - Spring Wind and Rain

The children listened to audio clips of wind and rain and tried to guess what they heard using the sentence starter 我听到……. They then looked at pictures of spring scenes and described them using 我看到……, practising careful listening and observation.

Using the measure words learned this week — 一棵, 一只, and 一朵 — the children extended their sentences by adding spring objects such as 我看见/听见 一棵/一只/一朵 树/小鸟/花. This helped them connect vocabulary with real-life spring scenes.

The children observed the shapes of the Chinese characters 风 (wind) and 雨 (rain) and used their imagination to think about how the character forms might relate to real natural scenes.
